<section class="cs-section">
  <h2 class="cs-label">Problem</h2>
  <p>A consulting firm focused on enterprise services was launching a new AI product business. Product development was outsourced to a Vietnam-based team. Domestic marketing had only three people working part-time across multiple responsibilities.</p>
  <p>There was no target customer definition, no clear positioning, no CRM or lead management infrastructure, and no product-to-marketing integration. The overseas development team was disconnected from domestic marketing, while the small team faced high operational demand with no system to support early sales activity.</p>
</section>

<section class="cs-section">
  <h2 class="cs-label">Work</h2>
  <p>Rather than implementing tools in isolation, we built a revenue foundation from scratch that integrated strategy design and system connectivity — treating GTM and infrastructure as one problem.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Positioning and GTM architecture — defined the target customer, market position, and acquisition logic from zero</li>
    <li>GTM alignment — created a shared strategy and decision framework across the organization within 2 months</li>
    <li>Product integration — used APIs to connect product usage data with HubSpot, enabling behavioral follow-up automation</li>
    <li>Lean automation design — built workflows that a three-person team could operate and maintain sustainably</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="cs-section">
  <h2 class="cs-label">Result</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>GTM strategy was defined and aligned across the organization in 2 months</li>
    <li>Product-connected marketing infrastructure was ready at launch</li>
    <li>A sustainable revenue engine was established for a three-person part-time team</li>
    <li>Automated workflows reduced operational load while keeping product usage data connected to HubSpot</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="cs-section">
  <h2 class="cs-label">Why it matters</h2>
  <p>Strategy without infrastructure doesn't launch. Infrastructure without strategy doesn't convert. This project designed both as one connected system — and that connection is what allowed a very small team to operate with the leverage of a much larger one.</p>
  <p>When product behavior feeds directly into CRM and marketing automation, the team doesn't need more people to follow up — they need the right structure. That's what this built.</p>
</section>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Founder's Message</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In Japan today, many companies find it difficult to create enough room to take on new challenges and invest in future growth. In a mature market, strategy alone is not enough. When teams are fragmented and execution is disconnected, it becomes much harder to turn direction into results.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>At the same time, my experiences abroad have broadened the way I see business, people, and collaboration. I have also met many people who genuinely care about Japan and want to build stronger connections with it. Those experiences shaped my belief that there is real value in bridging what Japan has to offer with the interest and opportunities coming from outside Japan.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Consilegy was built to support that bridge in practical ways. We do not stop at ideas. We step into the field, connect strategy with execution, and help clients move forward through systems, operations, and steady implementation.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<p>Seiko Yamaguchi founded Consilegy after working in IT consulting, marketing strategy, and project management.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
